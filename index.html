All children have special gifts, pediatric occupational therapist Kristine Hubner, helps unwrap them and gets them moving ahead!

At Kids Moving Ahead, we help children be successful at both home and school. Our mission is to help kids grow and develop to their full potential through innovative therapeutic intervention and family support.

Children have fun in an environment that is rich with sensory experiences while making gains in many areas of development:

  • Sensory processing, sensory integration and self regulation

  • Fine motor skills, including upper body and hand strengthening, refining pencil grasp, improving handwriting, scissor skills and more

  • Gross motor skills, including balance, motor planning, bilateral skills, core and postural strengthening and ball skills

  • Activities of Daily Living including: dressing, buttoning, shoe tying and feeding

  • Handwriting, primarily using Handwriting without Tears

  • Visual motor and visual perceptual skills

  • Play skills

  • Attention and organizational skills

  • Social and emotional development

Ms. Hubner's Orinda clinic is spacious and inviting. There is both a large indoor and outdoor area. The indoor area has numerous sensory integration-type swings, balance boards, ball pit, fine and visual motor activities. The outside has a large trampoline, zip line, rock wall and tree house. Intervention is highly individualized to ensure the greatest gains and maximum improvements possible.

Ms. Hubner has a wonderful, playful way with children. She is highly skilled at motivating children to try new things and move beyond their current abilities by designing and providing the "just right challenge." Comprehensive home programs and consultation to families are provided to maximize therapeutic effect. Ms. Hubner has experience working closely with teachers and other therapists to collaborate and provide the most effective treatment. Diagnoses served include, but are not limited to:

  • Sensory Integration Dysfunction/Sensory Processing Disorder

  • Gross, Fine and Visual Motor Concerns/Delays

  • ADD/ADHD

  • Incoordination, balance and motor planning concerns

  • Developmental Delay

  • Handwriting challenges/dysgraphia

  • Learning Disablity

  • Autistic Spectrum Disorder

  • CAPD

  • NVLD
